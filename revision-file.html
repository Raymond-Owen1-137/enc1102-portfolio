What Is Revision?

Actively engaging with feedback—peer comments, instructor notes—and making deliberate changes to improve structure, clarity, and argument strength.

Reflection

The evolution from my first draft to the Final Research Paper illustrates deliberate Revision. After receiving instructor and peer feedback—highlighting areas where my thesis needed tightening and my methodology needed clearer explanation—I reorganized sections for logical flow, refined my argument language, and clarified data-analysis procedures. I incorporated margin comments by explicitly addressing questions about framing definitions and chart readability. Each revision cycle involved targeted edits aimed at enhancing coherence, depth of analysis, and academic tone. This process demonstrates my capacity to interpret feedback constructively and to make strategic authorial decisions to strengthen my work.
